About

Reduction of child mortality is reflected in several of the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals and is a key indicator of human progress. The UN expects that by 2030, countries will end preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age, with all countries aiming to reduce under-5 mortality to at least as low as 25 per 1,000 live births. Parallel to the notion of child mortality is, of course, maternal mortality, which accounts for 295,000 deaths during and following pregnancy and childbirth (as of 2017). The vast majority of these deaths (94%) occurred in low-resource settings, and most could have been prevented. In light of the above, Cardiotocograms (CTGs) are a simple and cost-accessible option to assess fetal health, allowing healthcare professionals to take action to prevent child and maternal mortality. The equipment itself works by sending ultrasound pulses and reading the response, thus shedding light on fetal heart rate (FHR), fetal movements, uterine contractions, and more.

In this project, we have characteristics of Fetal Health as a dataset. The target variable of this dataset is Fetal Health. Since it is a multi-class classification, the classes are represented by ‘Normal’, ‘Pathological’, and ‘Suspect’.
